## Reflection

Started reading the Horus Heresy / Warhammer 30k franchise early in 2025 and finished the first 3 books after playing Space Marine 2. There's a sense of satisfaction you get from the excessively gore-y games where they don't try to steer away from it, but go all-in on it.

It certainly is a posterchild of the sci-fi genre that's on par with Star Wars when it comes to showcasing all the fantastic parts of the genre while handwaving the science-y stuff, so what is known as Soft Sci-Fi where a scientifically probable and somewhat believable explanation is unnecessary. Perhaps it's due to the fact that magic is widely accepted and truly exists in both franchises, even if they're named differently. E.g: the Force and Psionics.

I find these mixes of Fantasy and Sci-Fi awesome because I love both genres equally. It essentially takes the part of "wouldn't it be cool if it _always_ was like that?" of Fantasy and mixing it with "wouldn't it be cool if it _becomes_ like that?" of Sci-Fi, then mashes them into "wouldn't it be cool if it _was_ like that, but _is_ like this now?" that takes the enjoyment you take out of them, and instead of adding on top of each other, they end up getting multiplied.

The 4th book in the series, The Flight of the Eisenstein, is in half-read limbo where I will take them back up once I get in the mood for sci-fi again and will extend on this post further.

One thing I appreciate about Warhammer is that there's just too much media to consume related to it, and if I somehow finish Warhammer 40k, then I can just go for Warhammer Fantasy. For a kid that grew up watching and reading One Piece, Naruto and Dragon Ball where they seemed larger-than-life, like they could never end, and would keep on going forever, there's almost a nostalgic feel that you get from starting something that you'll probably never finish.
